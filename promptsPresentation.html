How to Write Good and Reusable Prompts for AI Chatbots

~45-Minute Non-Technical Presentation

Slide 1: Welcome

Introduction to the importance of writing good prompts and how this session will help you gain confidence using AI.

Slide 2: Why Prompts Matter

  • Clear prompts get better results.
  • Prompt writing is a valuable everyday skill.
  • Vague instructions confuse AI models.

Slide 3: Core Principles

  • Be clear and specific.
  • Assign a role/persona.
  • Use delimiters for clarity.
  • Provide examples.
  • Request step-by-step reasoning.
  • Test and refine.

Slide 4: How AI "Thinks"

  • LLMs generate text by predicting patterns based on your input.
  • They do not read minds—clarity is key.
  • Small wording changes can affect output greatly.

Slide 5: Prompt Example Walkthrough

Bad: "Summarize this report."

Better:

You are a friendly office assistant. 
Task: Summarize this report as a follow-up email to a colleague. 
Include key highlights, next steps. Use a polite tone. 
... 
Max: 150 words, use bullet points.

Slide 6: Designing Reusable Prompts

  • Use a template format.
  • Structure: role → task → context → output format.
  • Keep it modular and editable.

Slide 7: Advanced Tips

  • Negative prompting: tell AI what to avoid.
  • Ask for multiple options or reasoning paths.
  • Separate user data from instructions to avoid prompt injection.

Slide 8: Common Pitfalls

  • Vague or overcomplicated prompts.
  • Skipping prompt iteration.
  • Unclear output format.
  • Too many instructions at once.

Slide 9: Hacks from Real Users

  • Ask it to explain like you're 5.
  • “What’s the 80/20?” for summaries.
  • “Play devil’s advocate” for critical thinking.

Slide 10: Practice Exercise

Write a better version of: "Write a product description." Add persona, format, tone, audience, etc.

Slide 11: Prompt Checklist

  • Role defined?
  • Task clear?
  • Context given?
  • Output format?
  • Examples included?
  • Iterated/tested?

Slide 12: Q&A & Wrap-Up

Review key lessons and take questions.

Encourage practice and reuse of templates.
